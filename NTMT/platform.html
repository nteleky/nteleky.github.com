For 2013-2014, the plan is to focus on making our program better. That doesn't just mean help our program win more ballots - directly. That also means improving the way we work with each other, working to make sure that new members feel like they're a part of TMT more quickly, improving communication, making sure every member's voice is heard, and ultimately, making sure that mock trial is fun.

To make the program better, we have to focus on practical solutions to solve problems. Below, you'll can see each solution I'm proposing and would like to implement if elected co-president.

Anonymous Captain Evaluations
Right now, captains have no way of getting anonymous feedback about their performance as captains from their team. This makes it difficult to know if their captaining methods are working well for each individual, see if changes need to be made, or address other concerns.
Solution: Anonymous Captain Evaluations, twice a semester
  • How: Set up an online form and have TMT members rank their captains on several important attributes, then make any comments or suggestions to them. Allow members to submit this form anonymously.
  • The co-presidents are the only ones with direct access to the forms. After going over them, the co-presidents meet with each captain to talk about what their team had to say and how to improve.
  • Have evaluations in the middle of a semester (allows for captains to get feedback and immediately improve), and at the end of a semester (allows co-presidents to find the most effective captains for the following semester).
  • This way, all captains can get feedback on the best ways to lead their teams to make their teams more efficient, more effective, and ultimately, more fun.
TMT New Member Boot Camp
Right now, some new members don't feel like they've learned as much as they should've after first semester, not understanding how to argue/use objections, how to act as a witness structuring effective directs/crosses, or even what mock trial is even about.
Solution: TMT Boot Camp
  • This has been tossed around as an idea for a while, but it's time we made it happen: having a couple meetings with all the new members to talk about some mock basics:
    • Midlands Rules of Evidence - go deeper than the program-wide meeting
    • Cross Examination/Impeachment
    • Writing Good Directs
  • This isn't just for attorneys - the time will be split up to look at these topics from an attorney and witness perspective.
    • Maybe bring in some professors/grads from the drama dept?
  • Different members of the team will lead each extra meeting, so that new members can be exposed to different styles and members more quickly.
  • We want to encourage individual styles, so these meetings won't go into stylistic things -- just how things technically work.
  • This also allows new members to get to know eachother, integrating them in the program faster.
Mentor Program
New members are often intimidated by their captains because they feel the need early on to impress the two individuals who have been placed as their leaders. This makes it difficult for some to ask questions or ask for help.
Solution: Implement a Mentor Program (like Greek-life "Big-Little's")
  • Pair each new member with an upperclassman who is not their captain, not on their team, and not a co-president. (i.e. not someone in a power position that could intimidate new members.)
  • This mentor (like the big-little system in Greek life) is another resource for a new member and exists as someone they can go to to:
    • Ask questions about anything (objections, cross questions, etc)
    • Get a second-opinion about mock stuff before or after a practice
    • Learn how to handle tough situations in mock (Team problems, time management, etc.)
    • In general, get help adjusting to college/life at Tufts
  • This allows new members to feel more connected to the program on a more personal level and get to know other members of the program outside of their first invitational team.
  • This also creates new opportunities for upperclassmen to positively impact the program in a huge way.
Smart Scrimmages
Right now, invitational teams in particular work for about a month on their case before their first tournament, focusing in particular on things like objections and trying out case theory ideas and concepts. Typically though, teams dont' scrimmage eachother until the week of their first tournament, meaning that, if major flaws are found with case theory or major work is needed on any part of the case, there isn't a lot of time to make those changes.
Solution: Set up earlier scrimmages between each team
  • Set up scrimmages for ALL invitational teams 2-3 weeks into the invitational season (preferably 1.5-2 weeks before tournament weekend). This way a team can:
    • See early on if case theory/strategies will work or need to change
    • See how the case will play and get an idea of what needs to be worked on and have reasonably amounts of time to fix things before show time.
    • Expose new members to objection battles and new mock ideas/people earlier on.
  • Corollary: Set up scrimmages with other programs in fall semester to build relationships and introduce TMT members to other programs and other ways of mocking outside of tournament stress (when you have time to get new ideas/think of how to improve your own style)
Mumbo Jumbo: Improvements
Mumbo Jumbo has been impeccably run the last couple years, but right now, it's been just a few individuals doing everything. There are a few improvements that can be made.
  • All Hands On Deck - All TMT members should have a role in bringing Mumbo Jumbo to life, whether helping in Tab, being a runner, competing/byebusting, fundraising, getting food, finding judges, etc.
  • Last year we had one of the most competitive tournaments in the North East, and we should capitalize on that by getting stronger teams or teams from different regions of the country. Reaching out more would also improve relationships with other schools not just in our area, but throughout the country.
  • Make Mumbo Jumbo a leader in Mock Trial tournaments by digitizing the process:
    • Make pairings and room numbers available online with live updates.
    • Write and test a program designed to calculate ranks and/or do tab pairings.
    • Allow judges to judge electronically, eliminating paper. (Tougher for older judges, but something we could try experimenting with in a round)
    • The ideas are endless. We should try some!
  • Opening Ceremonies Video: We have the creative power to beat out UCLAssic 2013 video.. Let's do it. (Also it'd be fun).
Record Rounds/Scrimmages
Many members will never get to see what they look like or sound like while they're mocking. This makes it difficult for people to assess whether they're doing well or can improve , in areas like having a logical flow of questions, answering too quickly/slowly, speaking tone and speed, movement, etc.
Solution: Record scrimmages/rounds and review them
  • Put a camera in the corner of a room and do a brief review with your team to see how you're coming off as an attorney/witness and find new areas to improve.
  • Watching or listening to yourself is hard, but is easily one of the best ways to improve. You might think you have an amazing last question, or a great delivery for a joke on your direct -- but after watching it, you'll be able to see what worked and what didn't.
  • This also helps focus our program more towards the presentation aspects of MT and polish, as opposed to just and only case theory.
Digitize Ballots
Right now, if you want to review comments or see scores from a round, you have to first figure out who has the ballots, hope they haven't lost them, and then get them. After you're done with them, who should keep them? Where do they go? Alternatively, if you're watching a previously recorded TMT round (such as Tufts v Irvine from NCT 2012), you have no way of knowing what scored well or poorly because you don't have access to the ballots.
Solution: Digitize ballots by scanning and/or using excel
  • In today's day and age, we shouldn't have to keep a huge pile of old ballots from years ago, then sift thru them all to find a particular tournament or round and see how we did. There should be an electronic database so that these are easily accessible.
  • Co-presidents should retain discretion in allowing individuals to see ballots (as per the Constitution), but on the whole, these should be easily accessed.
  • Second-semester captains should be able to see ballots of their new teammates to see how their individual team members did in the previous semester and what they need to improve on according to judges (if there are any helpful comments).
Irvine: Beach Party/UCLA UCLAssic
Beach Party (Fall) and UCLAssic (Spring) are two of the most competitive invitationals in the country (on par with NYU's Downtown and UVA's GAMTI). We get invited to these every year, but largely because of funding, have to decline.
Solution: Create a plan to go to one of these by 2014-2015
  • Going to California for an invitational might seem ridiculous, but if there were a way we could make this happen financially, we absolutely should try it.
  • This gives at least one team exposure to an entirely different style of mock trial, one focused almost exclusively on immaculate presentation and being super confident. Having this exposure will help us prep for seeing these teams at Nationals and/or learn new ways to get better.
  • This also builds our relationship with west coast schools, which could prompt some to come to Mumbo Jumbo, raising the prestige of our tournament.
